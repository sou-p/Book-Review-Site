Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone

By J.K. Rowling

Genre: Fantasy

Rating: ★★★★☆

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone introduces us to Harry, an orphaned boy who discovers he's a wizard and attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. There, he finds friendship, battles evil, and uncovers the truth about his past and the dark wizard Voldemort. It's the magical start to one of the most beloved fantasy series in literature.

  • Harry Potter – The boy who lived, central hero of the story.
  • Hermione Granger – Brilliant Muggle-born witch, Harry's close friend.
  • Ron Weasley – Loyal best friend from a wizarding family.
  • Albus Dumbledore – Wise headmaster of Hogwarts.
  • Lord Voldemort – Dark wizard, antagonist seeking immortality.
  • Friendship & Loyalty – The trio’s bond highlights the importance of trust and teamwork.
  • Identity & Belonging – Harry's journey of self-discovery resonates with readers finding their place in the world.
  • Good vs. Evil – The eternal battle depicted through Voldemort and the resistance led by Dumbledore and Harry.
  • Choice & Destiny – Emphasized through Dumbledore’s assertion that our choices define us.

After being raised by his cruel aunt and uncle, Harry discovers on his 11th birthday that he is a wizard. He attends Hogwarts, a school for magic, where he learns about his heritage and makes lifelong friends. Along the way, he uncovers a secret involving the Philosopher’s Stone and faces trials that test his bravery, wit, and moral courage.

J.K. Rowling is a British author best known for writing the Harry Potter series. Her journey from living on welfare to becoming one of the most successful authors in history is as inspiring as her stories. She has also written novels under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith.
