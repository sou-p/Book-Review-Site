  Overview

Set in 1920s Bombay, "A Murder on Malabar Hill" follows Perveen Mistry, India’s first female lawyer, as she investigates a suspicious will and a murder in a wealthy Muslim household.

  Main Characters

  • Perveen Mistry – The pioneering female lawyer and investigator.
  • Mr. Farid – The family’s solicitor.
  • The Widows – Three women living in seclusion, central to the mystery.

  Prominent Themes

  • Women’s Rights: The challenges faced by women in early 20th-century India.
  • Justice and Tradition: The clash between modern law and traditional customs.
  • Family Secrets: The hidden motives and relationships within the household.

  Plot Summary

Perveen is called to review a will, but soon finds herself investigating a murder. As she navigates cultural barriers and family secrets, she must use her legal skills and intuition to uncover the truth.

  About the Author

Sujata Massey is an award-winning author known for her historical mysteries. Her work often features strong female protagonists and rich cultural settings.

  In-depth Review

"A Murder on Malabar Hill" is a compelling blend of mystery and historical fiction. Massey’s attention to detail and engaging protagonist make this a standout read.

Pros:

  • Strong Female Lead: Perveen is a memorable and inspiring character.
  • Rich Setting: Vividly depicts 1920s Bombay.

Cons:

  • Complex Plot: Multiple storylines may be challenging to follow.
  • Pacing: The story unfolds gradually.

Overall, "A Murder on Malabar Hill" is a must-read for fans of historical mysteries and strong female characters.
